## Weekly rhythm (so the 9 study hours don't feel repetitive)
- **Mon–Fri:** full stack as above (Technical → Stats → Tools → Project → Communication → Job Search → Review)
- **Saturday:** Study 1–5 = catch-up/deep work on whatever slipped during the week. Study 6–9 = mock interview practice + LinkedIn/networking.
- **Sunday:** Half-day. Light review only, no new topics. Full rest for body and job-search actions (applications can go out, but no new studying). This is not optional — recovery prevents burnout over 6 months.

## Hair & Skin (fits inside existing blocks, no new time needed)
- **Morning (5:30):** face wash + moisturizer + sunscreen. 30 seconds, done.
- **Bath (14:00–14:30):** shampoo every alternate day (not daily — over-washing dries hair/scalp), light oiling 2x/week before bath (apply 30 min before, e.g., during Study 5, then wash off).
- **Night (post 20:00 walk):** cleanse face, light moisturizer. Optional: comb hair gently, don't sleep with wet hair.

## Career phases (what fills "Core Technical," "Stats," "Tools," "Project" over the 6 months)
- **Weeks 1–3:** Rebuild SQL, Python/Pandas, Excel fundamentals from scratch — real coding, not quizzes.
- **Weeks 4–6:** Two full DA portfolio projects (raw data → clean → analyze → dashboard → insight write-up).
- **Weeks 6–8:** Resume + LinkedIn overhaul from the projects; start applying.
- **Months 3–6:** Layer in ML + deeper stats (DS-track) while continuing to apply/interview as DA.
